üìû Interview with Sarah Barrero üì±

Leah: How old were you when you got your first computer?

Sarah: I think I was 16.

Leah: What was your favorite piece of technology when you were growing up?

Sarah: Ummm my clock radio or CD player. I secretly liked my clock radio more because that's where I heard new music.

Leah: How has technology changed over your lifetime?

Sarah: Well in 1995 Bill Gates invented Windows 95, and then people started using the internet after around that time. It was all pretty fast. But the internet was slow and it sucked at first. There was no Google if you didn't know the address, you couldn't search. There wasn't Google til like 99. I used to think they should have a yellow pages for websites and then they made Google. I think technology has advanced in more ways than we even know about.

Leah: Have the changes you've seen in technology over your lifetime mostly helped or hurt society?

Sarah: I don't really know but I'm leaning towards helped. People were dumber and ignorant back then. Technology has made people a lot smarter.

Leah: What is your greatest hope for technology in the future?

Sarah: Oh man, there's no limit to the sci-fi fantasy. They could heal bodies, mind, do all our work for us. Computers could fix our every problem.

Leah: What is your greatest fear for technology in the future?

Sarah: Computers deciding not to work for us anymore. Singularity because full artificial intelligence can lead to human extinction.

Leah: Did you like it better when there was barely any technology or how it is today?

Sarah: Sometimes I miss the way things were for some reasons but overall I like it much better today.

Leah: What advice would you like to share with the younger generation about technology?

Sarah: Nothing gets deleted. Be careful what you say and do on social media.
